The Theorem

Statement: For any positive integer $n$:

$(a+b)^n = ^nC_0 a^n + ^nC_1 a^{n-1}b + \dots + ^nC_n b^n$.

Sigma Notation:

$(a+b)^n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} {^nC_k a^{n-k} b^k}$.

Observations:

Total terms = $n+1$.

Sum of powers of a and b is always $n$.

Key Cases
$(1+x)^n = ^nC_0 + ^nC_1 x + \dots + ^nC_n x^n$.
Coefficients are from Pascal's Triangle.

Expansion:

Expand $(x^2 + \frac{3}{x})^4$:

$= ^4C_0 (x^2)^4 + ^4C_1 (x^2)^3 (\frac{3}{x}) + ^4C_2 (x^2)^2 (\frac{3}{x})^2 + \dots$

$= x^8 + 4(x^6)(\frac{3}{x}) + 6(x^4)(\frac{9}{x^2}) + \dots$

$= x^8 + 12x^5 + 54x^2 + \dots$

General Term

Formula ($T_{r+1}$):

For $(a+b)^n$, the $(r+1)^{th}$ term is:

$T_{r+1} = ^nC_r a^{n-r} b^r$.

Finding Specific Terms:

To find coefficient of $x^m$, equate power of $x$ in general term to $m$.

Note
General term helps find "Independent of x" term (power = 0).

Find 4th term in $(x-2y)^{12}$:

$r=3$ (since $T_4 = T_{3+1}$).

$T_4 = ^{12}C_3 (x)^{12-3} (-2y)^3$.

$= \frac{12 \times 11 \times 10}{6} x^9 (-8y^3) = 220(-8) x^9 y^3 = -1760 x^9 y^3$.

Middle Terms

Case 1: n is Even:

Number of terms $n+1$ is Odd.

One middle term: $T_{n/2 + 1}$.

Case 2: n is Odd:

Number of terms $n+1$ is Even.

Two middle terms: $T_{(n+1)/2}$ and $T_{(n+1)/2 + 1}$.

Logic
Middle term has the greatest binomial coefficient ($^nC_{n/2}$).

Middle term of $(2x+3y)^8$:

$n=8$ (Even). Term is $T_{8/2+1} = T_5$.

$T_5 = ^8C_4 (2x)^4 (3y)^4$.

Tips & Tricks – Binomial

Coefficients:

$^nC_0 = 1, ^nC_n = 1$.

$^nC_1 = n$.

Divisibility Problems:

To show $6^n - 5n$ leaves remainder 1 when divided by 25:

Write $6^n = (1+5)^n = 1 + 5n + ^nC_2 5^2 + \dots$

$6^n - 5n = 1 + 25(\dots)$.

Approximations:

$(1.01)^{1000} \approx 1 + 1000(0.01) + \dots$

If $x$ is very small, $(1+x)^n \approx 1+nx$.

Shortcut
Total terms in $(x+a)^n + (x-a)^n$ is $(n/2)+1$ if n even.
