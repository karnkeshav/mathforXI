Subjective Questions

Q1. How many 3-digit numbers can be formed from the digits 1, 2, 3, 4 and 5 assuming that repetition of digits is allowed?

Show Answer
Solution: 3 places: _ _ _. Each place has 5 options.
Total ways = $5 \times 5 \times 5 = 125$.

Q2. Find the value of $n$ such that $^nP_5 = 42 \times ^nP_3$.

Show Answer
Solution: $\frac{n!}{(n-5)!} = 42 \frac{n!}{(n-3)!}$.
$(n-3)(n-4) = 42 \implies n^2-7n+12=42 \implies n^2-7n-30=0$.
$(n-10)(n+3)=0$. Since $n>0$, $n=10$.

Q3. In how many ways can a team of 3 boys and 3 girls be selected from 5 boys and 4 girls?

Show Answer
Solution: Selection of boys: $^5C_3$. Selection of girls: $^4C_3$.
Total ways: $^5C_3 \times ^4C_3 = 10 \times 4 = 40$.

Q4. How many words can be formed from the letters of the word "DAUGHTER" so that vowels always come together?

Show Answer
Solution: Vowels: A, U, E (3). Consonants: D, G, H, T, R (5).
Treat (AUE) as 1 unit. Total units: 5+1=6. Arrange 6 units: $6!$. Arrange vowels inside: $3!$.
Total: $6! \times 3! = 720 \times 6 = 4320$.
