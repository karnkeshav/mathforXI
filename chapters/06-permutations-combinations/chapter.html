Fundamental Principle of Counting

Multiplication Rule (AND):

If event A happens in $m$ ways AND event B happens in $n$ ways, then both happen in $m \times n$ ways.

Addition Rule (OR):

If event A happens in $m$ ways OR event B happens in $n$ ways (mutually exclusive), then either happens in $m + n$ ways.

Factorial ($n!$):

$n! = n \times (n-1) \times \dots \times 1$.

$0! = 1$.

Example
3 shirts, 2 pants.
Combinations: $3 \times 2 = 6$.

Permutations (Order Matters)

Definition: Arrangement of objects in a specific order.

Example: Ranking 1st, 2nd, 3rd.

Formula:

$^nP_r = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}$.

Arranging $n$ items in $r$ spots.

Repetition of Objects:

Arranging word "ROOT". 4 letters, 2 O's.

Ways = $\frac{4!}{2!} = \frac{24}{2} = 12$.

Key Idea
$^nP_n = n!$
$^nP_0 = 1$

Word Problems:

1. Word "ROSE": 4 distinct letters. $4! = 24$ ways.

2. Word "APPLE": 5 letters, 2 P's. $5!/2! = 120/2 = 60$ ways.

Combinations (Order Doesn't Matter)

Definition: Selection of objects where order is irrelevant.

Example: Selecting a team of 11 players.

Formula:

$^nC_r = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}$.

Also denoted as $\binom{n}{r}$.

Properties:

$^nC_r = ^nC_{n-r}$.

$^nC_0 = 1, ^nC_n = 1$.

Relation
$^nP_r = ^nC_r \times r!$
Selection $\times$ Arrangement = Permutation.

Selection Problems:

1. Select 3 from 5 friends: $^5C_3 = \frac{5 \times 4 \times 3}{3 \times 2 \times 1} = 10$.

2. Handshakes: 10 people shake hands. Order A-B is same as B-A. $^10C_2 = \frac{10 \times 9}{2} = 45$.

Tips & Tricks – P & C

Gap Method:

To ensure items are never together.

Place others first, then place items in gaps.

String Method:

To ensure items are always together.

Tie them as one unit. Arrange units. Then arrange inside the unit.

Dictionary Rank:

Fix first letter, count remaining arrangements. Move alphabetically.

Formula
Number of diagonals in n-sided polygon: $^nC_2 - n$.
