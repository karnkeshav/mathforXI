Mean Deviation

Definition: Average of absolute differences from Mean or Median.

$M.D.(\bar{x}) = \frac{\sum |x_i - \bar{x}|}{n}$.

Steps:

1. Calculate Mean ($\bar{x}$).

2. Find deviation $|x_i - \bar{x}|$ for each.

3. Find average of deviations.

Note
We take modulus to avoid sum becoming zero.

M.D. about Mean for 6, 7, 10, 12, 13, 4, 8, 12:

Sum = 72, n = 8. Mean $\bar{x} = 9$.

Deviations: $|6-9|=3, 2, 1, 3, 4, 5, 1, 3$.

Sum of Dev = 22.

M.D. = $22/8 = 2.75$.

Variance & Standard Deviation

Variance ($\sigma^2$): Average of squared deviations.

$\sigma^2 = \frac{\sum (x_i - \bar{x})^2}{n}$.

Standard Deviation ($\sigma$): Square root of Variance.

$\sigma = \sqrt{\text{Variance}}$.

Why Square?

To handle negative signs algebraically better than modulus.

Shortcut Formula
$\sigma^2 = \frac{\sum x_i^2}{n} - (\frac{\sum x_i}{n})^2$.
Mean of squares minus Square of mean.

S.D. of 2, 4, 6:

Mean = 4.

Deviations: -2, 0, 2.

Squares: 4, 0, 4.

Variance = $8/3 \approx 2.67$.

S.D. = $\sqrt{2.67} \approx 1.63$.

Coefficient of Variation (C.V.)

Definition: Comparison of variability relative to mean.

$C.V. = \frac{\sigma}{\bar{x}} \times 100$.

Interpretation:

Lower C.V. $\implies$ More consistent (stable).

Higher C.V. $\implies$ More variable.

Usage
Comparing units with different scales (e.g. height vs weight).

Compare two cricketers:

A: Mean=50, SD=5. CV = 10%.

B: Mean=20, SD=2. CV = 10%.

Both are equally consistent relative to their scoring ability.

Tips & Tricks – Statistics

Change of Origin/Scale:

Adding constant ($x+a$): Variance unchanged.

Multiplying constant ($ax$): SD becomes $|a|\sigma$, Variance $a^2\sigma^2$.

Calculation Trick:

Use Assumed Mean ($A$) and Step Deviation ($u_i$) for large numbers.

$\sigma_x = h \sigma_u$.

Combined Variance:

Merging two groups requires careful application of formula.

Standard Deviation is the root mean square deviation.
