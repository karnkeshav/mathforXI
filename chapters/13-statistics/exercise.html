Subjective Questions

Q1. Find the mean deviation about the mean for the data: 4, 7, 8, 9, 10, 12, 13, 17.

Show Answer
Solution: Sum = 80, n = 8. Mean $\bar{x} = 10$.
Deviations: $|4-10|=6, 3, 2, 1, 0, 2, 3, 7$.
Sum of Dev = 24. $M.D. = 24/8 = 3$.

Q2. Find the variance of first 10 multiples of 3.

Show Answer
Solution: Data: 3, 6, ..., 30. Mean $\bar{x} = \frac{3 \times 55}{10} = 16.5$.
Using $\sigma^2 = \frac{\sum x^2}{n} - (\bar{x})^2$.
$\sum (3k)^2 = 9 \sum k^2 = 9 \times 385 = 3465$.
$\sigma^2 = \frac{3465}{10} - (16.5)^2 = 346.5 - 272.25 = 74.25$.

Q3. If the coefficient of variation is 50% and standard deviation is 20, find the mean.

Show Answer
Solution: $C.V. = \frac{\sigma}{\bar{x}} \times 100$.
$50 = \frac{20}{\bar{x}} \times 100$.
$\bar{x} = \frac{2000}{50} = 40$.

Q4. The variance of 20 observations is 5. If each observation is multiplied by 2, find the new variance.

Show Answer
Solution: New Variance = $k^2 \times$ Old Variance.
$2^2 \times 5 = 4 \times 5 = 20$.
