Approach: Getting Close to a Value

Example 1: $f(x)=2x$, as $x \\to 2$ → value → 4
Example 2: $f(x)=x^2$, as $x \\to 3$ → value → 9
Example 3: $f(x)=5x$, as $x \\to 1$ → 5
Example 4: $f(x)=x^3$, as $x \\to 2$ → 8
Example 5: Approach does NOT require substitution
Formulas (Approach)
If $f(x)=ax+b$, then as $x \\to c$, value → $ac+b$

Limits: Algebraic Simplification

$(x^2-4)/(x-2) \\to 4$
$(x^2-9)/(x-3) \\to 6$
$(x^3-1)/(x-1) \\to 3$
$(x^2-a^2)/(x-a) \\to 2a$
Cancellation decides existence
Formulas (Limits)
$$\\lim_{x \\to a} \\frac{x^n-a^n}{x-a}=na^{n-1}$$

Problem Lab

Q: $\\lim_{x \\to 1} \\frac{x^3-1}{x-1}$

Step 1: Factor → $(x-1)(x^2+x+1)$

Step 2: Cancel $(x-1)$

Step 3: Substitute $x=1$

Answer: $3$

Checkpoints

First Principles

$[f(x+h)-f(x)]/h$
$h \\to 0$
$f(x)=x^2$
$f(x)=x$
Foundation of calculus
Formulas (First Principles)
$$\\lim_{h \\to 0} \\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}$$

Problem Lab

Q: Derivative of $x^2$

Step 1: $(x+h)^2-x^2$

Step 2: $2xh+h^2$

Step 3: Divide by $h$

Step 4: Limit $h \\to 0$

Answer: $2x$
