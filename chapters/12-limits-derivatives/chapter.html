Solving via Direct Substitution:

1. Check if the function is a simple polynomial or rational function.

2. Ensure the denominator does not become zero at the approach point.

3. Substitute the value of $x$ directly into the expression to find the limit.

Limits

$(x^2-4)/(x-2)$ → cancel → 4
$(x^2-9)/(x-3)$ → 6
$(x^3-1)/(x-1)$ → 3
All Formulas (Limits) $$\lim_{x\to a}\frac{x^n-a^n}{x-a}=na^{n-1}$$

Tackling $0/0$ Forms:

1. Factorization: If you see a polynomial, factor it to cancel terms like $(x-a)$.

2. Rationalization: If there are square roots, multiply by the conjugate.

3. Standard Formula: Identify if the problem matches $\frac{x^n-a^n}{x-a}$.

Trigonometric Limits

$\frac{\sin x}{x}\to1$
$\frac{1-\cos x}{x}\to0$
All Formulas (Trig Limits) $$\lim_{x\to0}\frac{\sin x}{x}=1 \quad | \quad \lim_{x\to0}\frac{\tan x}{x}=1 \quad | \quad \lim_{x\to0}\frac{1-\cos x}{x}=0$$

Mastering Trig Limits:

1. Angle Matching: Ensure the angle in $\sin(kx)$ matches the denominator $kx$.

2. Simplification: Use trig identities (like $1-\cos 2x = 2\sin^2 x$) to convert problems into standard sine limits.

Derivatives

$d/dx(x^2)=2x$
$d/dx(constant)=0$
All Formulas (Derivatives) $$\frac{d}{dx}(x^n)=nx^{n-1} \quad | \quad (uv)' = u'v + uv' \quad | \quad (\frac{u}{v})' = \frac{vu' - uv'}{v^2}$$

Applying Derivative Rules:

1. Product Rule: Use when two distinct functions of $x$ are multiplied (e.g., $x \cdot \sin x$).

2. Quotient Rule: Use for rational functions where $x$ is in both numerator and denominator.

First Principles

$[f(x+h)-f(x)]/h$
$$f'(x)=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}$$

1. Write the expression for $f(x+h)$.

2. Substitute $f(x+h)$ and $f(x)$ into the definition formula.

3. Simplify the numerator to factor out $h$.

4. Cancel $h$ and apply the limit $h \to 0$.

Tips & Tricks

For Complex Problems (NCERT Exemplar/Competitive)

1. Factor Detection: If a limit results in $0/0$, $(x-a)$ is a guaranteed factor of both top and bottom.

2. Conjugate Rule: For limits involving $\sqrt{a} - \sqrt{b}$, rationalizing the numerator is almost always the first step.

3. Trig Substitution: Convert $\tan x$, $\sec x$, or $\csc x$ into $\sin x$ and $\cos x$ immediately to simplify trigonometric limits.

4. L'Hôpital's Shortcut: For MCQs, if you get $0/0$, differentiate numerator and denominator separately until the $0/0$ is gone.

5. Product Rule Hack: $\frac{d}{dx}[c \cdot f(x)]$ is just $c \cdot f'(x)$. Do not waste time using the full product rule on constants.

Shortcut 1: $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1-\cos ax}{x^2} = \frac{a^2}{2}$. Very common in NCERT Exemplar.

Shortcut 2: If $f(x) = \frac{1}{x^n}$, then $f'(x) = \frac{-n}{x^{n+1}}$.

Shortcut 3: $\lim_{x \to a} \frac{x^n - a^n}{x^m - a^m} = \frac{n}{m} a^{n-m}$.
