Why Calculus Exists

Cars move. Prices change. Populations grow.

Algebra can compute totals and averages.

But the real question is:

What is happening right now?

The Limit Notation

Visual Trigonometry

Trigonometric Limit

For very small angles:

sin θ ≈ θ
lim (θ → 0) sinθ / θ = 1
✔ This single limit powers most of calculus
Motion → Slope

Derivative as Instantaneous Change

  • Speed is derivative of distance
  • Slope is derivative of a curve
✔ Derivative = limit of average change

Checkpoint 2

Derivative measures:

Definition

Derivative from First Principles

Average change = [ f(x+h) − f(x) ] / h

Letting h → 0 converts average change into instant change:

Derivative = lim (h → 0) [ f(x+h) − f(x) ] / h
✔ This is the foundation of all derivative formulas
