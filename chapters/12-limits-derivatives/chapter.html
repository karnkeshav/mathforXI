Limits: Algebraic Foundations

Example 1: $(x^2−4)/(x−2)$

Cancel → $x+2 \to 4$

Example 2: $(x^2−9)/(x−3) \to 6$

Comprehensive Formula Bank:
  • General Power Rule: $$\lim_{x \to a} \frac{x^n - a^n}{x - a} = na^{n-1}$$
  • Sum/Difference Rule: $$\lim_{x \to a} [f(x) \pm g(x)] = \lim_{x \to a} f(x) \pm \lim_{x \to a} g(x)$$
  • Product/Quotient Rule: $$\lim_{x \to a} [f(x) \cdot g(x)] = \lim_{x \to a} f(x) \cdot \lim_{x \to a} g(x)$$

NCERT Basic: Evaluate $\lim_{x \to 2} (x^3 - 4x + 1)$.
Use: Direct Substitution.

Exemplar Standard: Evaluate $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sqrt{1+x} - 1}{x}$.
Use: Rationalization of the numerator.

Exemplar Complex: Evaluate $\lim_{x \to 1} \frac{x^{15}-1}{x^{10}-1}$.
Use: Divide Numerator/Denominator by $(x-1)$ and apply $\frac{na^{n-1}}{ma^{m-1}}$.

Trigonometric & Special Limits

$\sin\theta/\theta \to 1$
$\tan\theta/\theta \to 1$
Comprehensive Formula Bank:
  • Standard Identity: $$\lim_{\theta \to 0} \frac{\sin \theta}{\theta} = 1$$
  • Tangent Identity: $$\lim_{\theta \to 0} \frac{\tan \theta}{\theta} = 1$$
  • Cosine Identity: $$\lim_{\theta \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos \theta}{\theta} = 0$$
  • Exemplar Extra: $$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{e^x - 1}{x} = 1$$

NCERT Basic: $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin 4x}{x}$.
Use: Multiply and divide by 4.

Standard: $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x^2}$.
Use: Trig identity $1-\cos x = 2\sin^2(x/2)$.

Exemplar Complex: $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{e^{\sin x} - 1}{x}$.
Use: Change of variable or chain-limit.

Derivatives: Product & Quotient Rules

$d/dx (x^2)=2x$
$d/dx (constant)=0$
Comprehensive Formula Bank:
  • Power Rule: $$\frac{d}{dx}(x^n) = nx^{n-1}$$
  • Product Rule: $$\frac{d}{dx}[u \cdot v] = u \frac{dv}{dx} + v \frac{du}{dx}$$
  • Quotient Rule: $$\frac{d}{dx}[\frac{u}{v}] = \frac{v \frac{du}{dx} - u \frac{dv}{dx}}{v^2}$$
  • Trig: $\frac{d}{dx}\sin x = \cos x$, $\frac{d}{dx}\cos x = -\sin x$

NCERT Basic: Differentiate $x^2 + 5x - 6$.
Use: Power Rule term-by-term.

Standard: Differentiate $x \cdot \sin x$.
Use: Product Rule.

Exemplar Complex: Differentiate $\frac{ax + b}{cx + d}$.
Use: Quotient Rule.
