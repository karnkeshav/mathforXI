Angles & Measurement

Two Units:

Degrees: $360^\circ$ in a circle.

Radians: $2\pi$ radians in a circle.

Conversion:

$\pi \text{ rad} = 180^\circ$

$1 \text{ rad} \approx 57^\circ 16'$

$1^\circ \approx 0.01746 \text{ rad}$

Arc Length ($l$) and Radius ($r$):

Angle $\theta$ (in radians) $= l/r$.

$l = r\theta$.

Formula Bank
Deg to Rad: $\times \frac{\pi}{180}$
Rad to Deg: $\times \frac{180}{\pi}$

Conversions:

1. $40^\circ 20'$ to radians: $20' = (20/60)^\circ = (1/3)^\circ$. Total $40.33^\circ$. Multiply by $\pi/180$.

2. $6$ radians to degrees: $6 \times \frac{180}{\pi} = \frac{1080}{22/7} \approx 343^\circ 38' 11''$.

Trigonometric Functions (Unit Circle)

Definition: Consider a unit circle (radius 1).

Coordinates of a point $P$ on circle at angle $x$: $(\cos x, \sin x)$.

Signs in Quadrants (ASTC):

I: All Positive.

II: Sin Positive (S).

III: Tan Positive (T).

IV: Cos Positive (C).

Periodicity:

$\sin(2n\pi + x) = \sin x$.

$\cos(2n\pi + x) = \cos x$.

Values
$\sin(\pi) = 0, \cos(\pi) = -1$
$\sin(3\pi/2) = -1, \cos(3\pi/2) = 0$

Finding Values:

1. $\cos(-1710^\circ)$: $\cos(-x) = \cos x$. $1710 = 4 \times 360 + 270$. So $\cos(270^\circ) = 0$.

2. $\sin(31\pi/3)$: $31\pi/3 = 10\pi + \pi/3$. Period is $2\pi$. Equivalent to $\sin(\pi/3) = \sqrt{3}/2$.

Compound Angles & Identities

Sum & Difference:

$\sin(x+y) = \sin x \cos y + \cos x \sin y$

$\cos(x+y) = \cos x \cos y - \sin x \sin y$

Double Angle:

$\sin 2x = 2\sin x \cos x$

$\cos 2x = \cos^2 x - \sin^2 x = 2\cos^2 x - 1$

Sum to Product:

$\sin A + \sin B = 2\sin(\frac{A+B}{2})\cos(\frac{A-B}{2})$

Identity Box
$\tan(x+y) = \frac{\tan x + \tan y}{1 - \tan x \tan y}$
$1 + \cos 2x = 2\cos^2 x$
$1 - \cos 2x = 2\sin^2 x$

Trigonometric Equations

Principal Solution:

Solutions in the interval $[0, 2\pi)$.

General Solution:

$\sin x = \sin y \implies x = n\pi + (-1)^n y$

$\cos x = \cos y \implies x = 2n\pi \pm y$

$\tan x = \tan y \implies x = n\pi + y$

Tips
Always find the smallest positive angle $\alpha$ first.
Check quadrant to find $y$.

Solving Equations:

1. $\sin x = \frac{1}{2}$: Principal: $\pi/6, 5\pi/6$. General: $n\pi + (-1)^n \frac{\pi}{6}$.

2. $\tan x = -1$: $\tan$ negative in II and IV. II Quadrant: $\pi - \pi/4 = 3\pi/4$. General: $n\pi + 3\pi/4$.

Tips & Tricks – Trigonometry

ASTC Rule:

All Silver Tea Cups (All, Sin, Tan, Cos).

Angle Reduction:

  • $n\pi \pm \theta$: Function remains same.
  • $(2n+1)\pi/2 \pm \theta$: Function changes (sin $\leftrightarrow$ cos).

Half Angle Tricks:

Use $1-\cos \theta = 2\sin^2(\theta/2)$ and $1+\cos \theta = 2\cos^2(\theta/2)$ often in calculus.
