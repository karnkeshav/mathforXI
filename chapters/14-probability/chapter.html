Axiomatic Approach

Definition: $P(E)$ is a number such that:

1. $0 \le P(E) \le 1$.

2. $P(S) = 1$.

3. If A and B disjoint, $P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B)$.

Classical Formula (Equally Likely):

$P(E) = \frac{\text{Number of outcomes in E}}{\text{Total outcomes in S}} = \frac{n(E)}{n(S)}$.

Probability of 'Not A'
$P(A') = 1 - P(A)$.

Prob. of getting sum 7 with 2 dice:

$n(S) = 36$.

$E = \{(1,6), (2,5), (3,4), (4,3), (5,2), (6,1)\}$. $n(E)=6$.

$P(E) = 6/36 = 1/6$.

Algebra of Probability

Addition Theorem:

$P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)$.

Prob(A or B) = Prob(A) + Prob(B) - Prob(Both).

Mutually Exclusive Events:

$P(A \cap B) = 0$.

$P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B)$.

Logic
"OR" means Union ($\cup$). "AND" means Intersection ($\cap$).

Tips & Tricks – Probability

At Least One:

$P(\text{At least one}) = 1 - P(\text{None})$.
