Subjective Questions

Q1. A coin is tossed twice. What is the probability that at least one tail occurs?

Show Answer
Solution: $S = \{HH, HT, TH, TT\}$. $E = \{HT, TH, TT\}$.
$P(E) = 3/4$.

Q2. Two dice are thrown. The events A, B and C are as follows:
A: getting an even number on the first die.
B: getting an odd number on the first die.
C: getting the sum of the numbers on the dice $\le 5$.
Describe the event A and B.

Show Answer
Solution: A: first die even. B: first die odd.
Intersection $A \cap B = \phi$ (A number cannot be even and odd). They are mutually exclusive.

Q3. If P(A) = 3/5 and P(B) = 1/5, find P(A or B) if A and B are mutually exclusive events.

Show Answer
Solution: $P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) = 3/5 + 1/5 = 4/5$.

Q4. A card is selected from a pack of 52 cards. Find the probability that the card is an ace of spades.

Show Answer
Solution: Only 1 Ace of Spades. Total 52.
$P(E) = 1/52$.
