Subjective Questions

Q1. Find the equation of the line passing through $(-4, 3)$ with slope $1/2$.

Show Answer
Solution: $y - 3 = \frac{1}{2}(x - (-4))$.
$2(y-3) = x+4 \implies 2y-6 = x+4 \implies x-2y+10=0$.

Q2. Find the distance of the point $(3, -5)$ from the line $3x - 4y - 26 = 0$.

Show Answer
Solution: $d = \frac{|3(3) - 4(-5) - 26|}{\sqrt{3^2 + (-4)^2}}$.
$d = \frac{|9 + 20 - 26|}{5} = \frac{3}{5}$.

Q3. Find the angle between the lines $y - \sqrt{3}x - 5 = 0$ and $\sqrt{3}y - x + 6 = 0$.

Show Answer
Solution: Line 1: $m_1 = \sqrt{3}$. Line 2: $m_2 = 1/\sqrt{3}$.
$\tan \theta = |\frac{m_2 - m_1}{1 + m_1 m_2}| = |\frac{1/\sqrt{3} - \sqrt{3}}{1 + 1}| = |\frac{-2/\sqrt{3}}{2}| = |-1/\sqrt{3}|$.
$\theta = 30^\circ$.

Q4. Find the equation of a line perpendicular to $x - 2y + 3 = 0$ and passing through $(1, -2)$.

Show Answer
Solution: Slope of given line $m = 1/2$. Slope of perpendicular $m' = -2$.
Eq: $y - (-2) = -2(x - 1) \implies y+2 = -2x+2 \implies 2x+y=0$.
