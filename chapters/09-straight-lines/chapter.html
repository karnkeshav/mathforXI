Slope (Gradient)

Definition: Inclination $\theta$ with positive x-axis.

Slope $m = \tan \theta$.

Two Points Formula:

$m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$.

Parallel & Perpendicular:

Parallel: $m_1 = m_2$.

Perpendicular: $m_1 m_2 = -1$.

Angle Between Lines
$\tan \theta = |\frac{m_2 - m_1}{1 + m_1 m_2}|$.

Find slope of line through (1, 2) and (4, 5):

$m = \frac{5-2}{4-1} = \frac{3}{3} = 1$.

Angle: $\tan \theta = 1 \implies \theta = 45^\circ$.

Forms of Equation

Point-Slope Form:

$y - y_0 = m(x - x_0)$.

Two-Point Form:

$y - y_1 = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}(x - x_1)$.

Slope-Intercept Form:

$y = mx + c$ (y-intercept c).

Intercept Form:

$\frac{x}{a} + \frac{y}{b} = 1$ (x-int a, y-int b).

Normal Form
$x \cos \omega + y \sin \omega = p$
(p is perpendicular distance from origin).

General Equation

Standard Form: $Ax + By + C = 0$.

Reduction to Slope-Intercept:

$By = -Ax - C \implies y = (-\frac{A}{B})x + (-\frac{C}{B})$.

Slope $m = -A/B$.

Reduction to Intercept Form:

$Ax + By = -C \implies \frac{x}{-C/A} + \frac{y}{-C/B} = 1$.

Key Fact
Any linear equation in x and y represents a straight line.

$A=3, B=4$. Slope $m = -A/B = -3/4$.

Distance Formulas

Point from Line:

Distance of $(x_1, y_1)$ from $Ax + By + C = 0$:

$d = \frac{|Ax_1 + By_1 + C|}{\sqrt{A^2 + B^2}}$.

Distance between Parallel Lines:

Lines: $Ax + By + C_1 = 0$ and $Ax + By + C_2 = 0$.

$d = \frac{|C_1 - C_2|}{\sqrt{A^2 + B^2}}$.

Note
Make sure coefficients of x and y are same for parallel distance formula.

Distance of (1, 1) from $3x - 4y + 1 = 0$:

$d = \frac{|3(1) - 4(1) + 1|}{\sqrt{3^2 + (-4)^2}} = \frac{|0|}{5} = 0$.

Point lies on the line.

Tips & Tricks – Straight Lines

Collinearity:

Three points are collinear if Slope(AB) = Slope(BC).

Or Area of triangle is 0.

Concurrent Lines:

Three lines meet at a point if determinant of coefficients is 0.

Image of a Point:

Image $(h, k)$ of $(x_1, y_1)$ in line $Ax+By+C=0$:

$\frac{h-x_1}{A} = \frac{k-y_1}{B} = -2\frac{Ax_1+By_1+C}{A^2+B^2}$.

Shortcut
Area of triangle formed by line $Ax+By+C=0$ with axes is $\frac{1}{2} |\frac{C^2}{AB}|$.
