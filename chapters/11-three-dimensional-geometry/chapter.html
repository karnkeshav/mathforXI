Coordinates & Octants

3 Axes: X, Y, Z. They are mutually perpendicular.

3 Planes: XY, YZ, ZX planes.

8 Octants:

Determined by signs of x, y, z.

I: (+, +, +), II: (-, +, +), ..., VIII: (+, -, -).

Coordinates of Point P: $(x, y, z)$.

On X-axis: $(x, 0, 0)$.

In XY-plane: $(x, y, 0)$.

Distance Formula

Formula:

Distance between $P(x_1, y_1, z_1)$ and $Q(x_2, y_2, z_2)$:

$PQ = \sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2 + (y_2-y_1)^2 + (z_2-z_1)^2}$.

Distance from Origin:

$OP = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}$.

Analogy
It's just the 2D formula with an extra $z^2$ term added inside square root.

Distance between $(1, -3, 4)$ and $(-4, 1, 2)$:

$\Delta x = -5, \Delta y = 4, \Delta z = -2$.

$d = \sqrt{(-5)^2 + 4^2 + (-2)^2} = \sqrt{25 + 16 + 4} = \sqrt{45} = 3\sqrt{5}$.

Section Formula

Internal Division ($m:n$):

$x = \frac{mx_2 + nx_1}{m+n}$

$y = \frac{my_2 + ny_1}{m+n}$

$z = \frac{mz_2 + nz_1}{m+n}$

External Division ($m:n$):

Replace $+$ with $-$ in numerator and denominator.

Midpoint Formula:

$(\frac{x_1+x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1+y_2}{2}, \frac{z_1+z_2}{2})$.

Centroid of Triangle
$G = (\frac{x_1+x_2+x_3}{3}, \frac{y_1+y_2+y_3}{3}, \frac{z_1+z_2+z_3}{3})$.

Midpoint of $(2, 3, 4)$ and $(4, 1, -2)$:

$x = (2+4)/2 = 3$.

$y = (3+1)/2 = 2$.

$z = (4-2)/2 = 1$.

Point: $(3, 2, 1)$.

Tips & Tricks – 3D Geometry

Collinearity:

Three points A, B, C are collinear if:

$AB + BC = AC$ (using distance formula).

Distance from Axes:

Distance of $(x, y, z)$ from X-axis is $\sqrt{y^2+z^2}$.

From Y-axis is $\sqrt{x^2+z^2}$.

From Z-axis is $\sqrt{x^2+y^2}$.

Coordinates of Centroid:

Simply average the coordinates of vertices.

Shortcut
To show parallelogram, show midpoints of diagonals coincide.
