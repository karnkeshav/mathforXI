Subjective Questions

Q1. Find the distance between the points $P(1, -3, 4)$ and $Q(-4, 1, 2)$.

Show Answer
Solution: $PQ = \sqrt{(-4-1)^2 + (1-(-3))^2 + (2-4)^2}$.
$= \sqrt{(-5)^2 + 4^2 + (-2)^2} = \sqrt{25+16+4} = \sqrt{45} = 3\sqrt{5}$.

Q2. Show that the points $A(1, 2, 3), B(-1, -2, -1), C(2, 3, 2)$ and $D(4, 7, 6)$ are the vertices of a parallelogram.

Show Answer
Solution: Find midpoints of diagonals AC and BD.
Midpoint AC: $(1.5, 2.5, 2.5)$. Midpoint BD: $(1.5, 2.5, 2.5)$.
Since midpoints coincide, ABCD is a parallelogram.

Q3. Find the coordinates of the point which divides the line segment joining $(1, -2, 3)$ and $(3, 4, -5)$ in ratio $2:3$ internally.

Show Answer
Solution: $x = \frac{2(3)+3(1)}{5} = 9/5$.
$y = \frac{2(4)+3(-2)}{5} = 2/5$.
$z = \frac{2(-5)+3(3)}{5} = -1/5$.
Point: $(9/5, 2/5, -1/5)$.

Q4. Find the octant in which $(-3, 1, -2)$ lies.

Show Answer
Solution: Signs are (-, +, -). This is Octant VI.
