Algebraic Solutions

Rules:

1. Add/Subtract same number: Sign stays same.

2. Multiply/Divide by positive: Sign stays same.

3. Multiply/Divide by negative: Sign reverses.

Example:

$3x - 5 < 10 \implies 3x < 15 \implies x < 5$.

$-2x > 6 \implies x < -3$ (Sign flipped!).

Interval Notation:

$x < 5 \implies (-\infty, 5)$.

$x \ge 2 \implies [2, \infty)$.

Key Idea
Strict ($<, >$): Open circles / dotted lines.
Slack ($\le, \ge$): Closed circles / solid lines.

Solving:

1. $5x-3 < 3x+1$: $2x < 4 \implies x < 2$. Interval: $(-\infty, 2)$.

2. $\frac{x}{3} > \frac{x}{2} + 1$: Multiply by 6: $2x > 3x + 6$. $-x > 6 \implies x < -6$.

Systems of Inequalities

Definition: More than one inequality to satisfy simultaneously.

Example: $x \ge 0, y \ge 0, x+y \le 4$.

Solution Region:

The common shaded area (overlap) of all inequalities.

This region is often a polygon.

Corner Points
Vertices of the solution region are important for optimization (Class XII).

Tips & Tricks – Inequalities

Sign Flipper:

Watch out for multiplying/dividing by negative numbers. It is the #1 mistake.

Line Type:

Check strictness ($<$) vs slack ($\le$) before drawing the line.

Word Problems:

"At least" $\implies \ge$.

"At most" $\implies \le$.

"More than" $\implies >$.

Double Inequality
$2 < 3x-4 \le 5$
Add 4 everywhere: $6 < 3x \le 9$
Divide by 3: $2 < x \le 3$.
